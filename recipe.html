Vanilla Cake Recipe

This classic vanilla cake is light, fluffy, and moist with a rich buttery flavor.

Made with simple pantry ingredients, it bakes into a golden, tender crumb that

pairs perfectly with creamy buttercream frosting.

for birthdays, celebrations, or a simple homemade treat.

Recipe Information

  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 30-35 minutes
  • Total Time: 50-55 minutes
  • Servings: 8-10 slices
  • Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups (190g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 ¾ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ cup (120ml) milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven. Set your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour an 8-inch round cake pan (or line it with parchment paper) so the cake won’t stick.
  2. Cream the butter and sugar. In a large mixing bowl, beat together ½ cup softened butter and 1 cup sugar until the mixture becomes light and fluffy. This helps incorporate air, giving your cake a soft texture.
  3. Add the eggs and vanilla. Crack in 2 large eggs, adding them one at a time. Beat well after each egg so they blend smoothly. Stir in 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract for flavor.
  4. Prepare the dry ingredients. In a separate bowl, mix together 1 ½ cups of all-purpose flour and 1 ¾ teaspoons of baking powder. This ensures the baking powder is evenly distributed throughout the flour.
  5. Combine wet and dry mixtures. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter-sugar mixture. Alternate between adding the flour mixture and ½ cup milk, mixing gently each time until everything is combined into a smooth batter.
  6. Pour the batter into the pan. Transfer the batter to your prepared cake pan, spreading it out evenly with a spatula.
  7. Bake the cake. Place the pan in the oven and bake for 30–40 minutes. The cake is ready when a toothpick or knife inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Cool the cake. Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then turn it out onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing or frosting.

Helpful Tip

For an extra moist cake, you can substitute half of the milk with sour cream or yogurt. This adds a slight tanginess and enhances the cake's texture.

Storage tips: Store in an airtight container at room temperature for 2–3 days, or refrigerate for up to a week. Optional variations: Optional variations: You can add chocolate chips, a layer of jam, or frost with buttercream for extra flavor.
